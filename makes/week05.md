# Week 5 – GIF & Remix Culture
Student Name: Duc Tran

Title: I MADE THIS.

Tools Used: ChatGPT (GIF generation), GIF export tools

## The Artifact (Description)

The GIF is a short looping animation built around the repeated phrase “I MADE THIS.” The
design is minimal: a clean white card, grayscale background, repeated “REMIX / LOOP” texture,
and a frame counter. Across the 8-frame loop, the main phrase repeats with subtle visual shifts.
The GIF does not rely on dramatic motion. Instead, it uses repetition and small changes to
make the same statement feel different over time. The loop repeats continuously, so the phrase
gradually shifts from a confident ownership claim to something more uncertain, performative, or
ironic.

## Reflection (≈250 words)
Making this GIF helped me understand that looping changes meaning even when the content
stays almost the same. First, the phrase “I MADE THIS.” reads like a simple statement of
authorship. But once it repeats in a loop, it starts to feel less stable. The repetition makes it
sound more like a defense, a performance, or even a joke. Instead of confirming authorship, the
loop makes the viewer question why the claim has to be repeated.

This changed how I think about GIFs as a form. The GIF is not just a short video clip but a
format where repetition becomes the main meaning-making tool. Because there is no clear
beginning or ending, the phrase keeps returning without resolution. That circular time structure
creates tension and makes the viewer pay attention to tone, not just words.

This also connects to remix culture and AI collaboration. The phrase “I made this” carries
cultural baggage online because digital content is constantly reposted, edited, and detached
from its original context. In the AI era, that tension becomes stronger because tools can
generate and transform media so quickly. For this project, AI helped with production and
iteration, but the meaning came from my choices: what to repeat, how to pace it, and how to
frame the message visually.

This assignment showed me that GIFs can be critical, not just entertaining. Meaning emerges
through repetition, selection, and circulation.
