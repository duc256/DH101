## Voyant Analysis (Summary)
Voyant showed that the most frequent words in my excerpt were freedom (13), ring (12), let (11),
negro (10), and dream (10). The word cloud also made words like today, satisfied, years, and
day stand out, which suggests the speech combines urgency, repetition, and historical framing.

The trends and collocations views helped me see how King builds emphasis. In the trends
graph for “freedom,” the word appears much more strongly near the end of the excerpt, which
matches the “let freedom ring” section. The collocates panel also showed that freedom appears
near words like ring, mountain, hilltops, Rockies, and Lookout, which reveals how the speech
connects freedom to national geography and repeated phrasing.

Voyant helped me identify patterns quickly and gave me strong evidence for repetition and
structure. However, it did not explain why those patterns are persuasive or emotionally powerful.

## GPT Analysis (Summary)
Prompt: 

“Provide a rhetorical analysis of Martin Luther King Jr.’s I Have a Dream excerpt, focusing on
repetition, tone, structure, and audience.”

GPT explained that the speech uses repetition as a core persuasive strategy, especially through
“we cannot be satisfied,” “I have a dream,” and “let freedom ring.” It interpreted the speech as
moving from injustice and urgency toward hope and a collective vision of freedom.

GPT also identified the tone as both urgent and hopeful, and it explained how King uses
imagery, rhythm, and moral language to strengthen his message. The analysis was useful and
smooth, but like the model example, it sounded authoritative without automatically showing
textual evidence unless I specifically asked for line-based support.

## Comparison
Voyant treats the speech as a set of words, frequencies, and patterns. It is strong at showing
repetition, distribution, and collocations, and it is more transparent about how it gets its results.
GPT treats the speech as a rhetorical whole and offers interpretation about theme, tone, and
persuasion.

In my case, Voyant clearly showed the pattern of freedom clustering near the end and the
repeated phrase network around “freedom ring.” GPT helped explain what that means
rhetorically: the speech builds toward a climactic, national call for justice.
Voyant is transparent but limited. GPT is insightful but can feel authoritative without showing its
process. Using both gave me a stronger reading than using either one alone.

## Reflection (≈230 words)
This assignment helped me see that machine reading changes depending on the method.
Voyant reads the speech by counting and visualizing language. That made repetition very
visible in King’s speech, especially words like freedom, ring, and dream. The trends graph was
also useful because it showed that freedom becomes much more concentrated near the end,
which helped me notice how the speech builds toward a climax. In that way, Voyant gave me
evidence for structure, not just word count.

GPT reads differently. It treated the speech as a persuasive argument and explained tone,
audience, and rhetorical purpose. It helped me understand how the repeated phrases work
together: first naming injustice, then imagining a better future, and finally calling for freedom
across the nation. That kind of interpretation is something Voyant cannot really do.

At the same time, GPT can sound more certain than it should. It gives fluent interpretations, but
it does not automatically show how it arrived at them unless I ask for evidence from the text.
Voyant is more trustworthy for frequency and collocation data, but it cannot explain meaning on
its own. What we gain from machine reading is speed, pattern recognition, and new ways to
notice structure. What we lose is the human process of judgment if we rely on the tools too
much. For me, the best approach is using both while still doing my own close reading.
