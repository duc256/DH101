## Sustainability & Ethics

# My Research
Make 11 project made me think about AI in a more real way. Before, I mostly saw AI as something helpful and convenient. Now I see that it also depends on data centers, electricity, water, and hardware, which means it has real environmental costs. What surprised me most is how invisible all of this feels when we use AI in everyday life.

# Data Sources
For the make 11 project, I used sources on AI training emissions, per-prompt energy use, and data-center electricity demand. I mainly drew from Strubell, Ganesh, and McCallum, Google Cloud, and the International Energy Agency. I also used ideas from class, especially the idea that AI is not just digital but also material and extractive.

# Personal Reflection
The project honestly made me feel more conflicted about AI. I still use it a lot for school, especially for brainstorming, explaining hard ideas, and helping me get started when I am stuck. At the same time, I do not feel as comfortable thinking of it as a harmless tool anymore. It is useful, but that usefulness comes with environmental costs that are easy to ignore because they happen somewhere else.

I do not think I am going to stop using AI, and I do not want to pretend that I will. What I can do is use it more intentionally. I want to be more aware of when I actually need it and when I am just using it because it is faster or easier.

# Systemic vs. Individual
I think individuals do have some responsibility, but not the most responsibility. I can choose to use AI less carelessly, but I do not control where data centers are built, how much energy they use, or where the waste goes. That is why I think companies and policymakers carry much more responsibility than ordinary users.

# Final Reflection
My biggest takeaway is that AI is not just a smart tool. It is also part of a larger system that uses resources and creates unequal costs. For me, the ethical question is not only what AI can do for us, but also what kind of environmental trade-offs we are accepting when we use it.
